Intellectually, emotionally and conversationally, I abhor lists of all kinds. Top ten this or that makes me feel top-notch geeky. Still, I like sven more than I hate lists so, per his request, here is my top 25 songs. Except. He requested something like 25 favorite songs of all time but below are the 25 most played songs from my iTunes. They are my favorite insomuch as they have been played most often. That is all. Do I sound like a curmudgeon? Or a snob? Don't answer! List below the fold.

  • 25: A Wolf At The Door (It Girl. Rag Doll) by Radiohead. The whole album is amazing. This is one of the best.
  • 24: The Good, The Bad and the Chutney by DJ Shadow and Dan the Automater. In terms of Bollywood remixes, Bombay The Hard Way is the best. This song has Amitabh Bachan's voice. Cannot be beaten.
  • 23: Los Angeles, I'm Yours by The Decembrists. I lived in LA. I do not like LA. This song makes me want to go back to LA.
  • 22: Hurt by Johnny Cash. One of the great covers of all time. Surpasses my criteria that a cover should re-invent the song. This is the saddest example.
  • 21: Naufrago by Ibrahim Ferrer. Buena Vista Club's popularity gave the great Ibrahim Ferrer a new audience. He was introduced to me by my late friend Asad. Everytime I listen to this CD, I remember him.
  • 20: Banks of Ohio by Snakefarm. No clue why this is here. Not a bad song though the British need to stop fixating on Bluegrass from the 20s.
  • 19: Piano Concerto No. 2 in G Minor, Op. 22 by Camille Saint-SaÃÅ½ns. Must be Ms. Sepoy's work, this one.
  • 18: Deep Red Bells by Neko Case. Neko has a voice like Patsy Cline and the reverb here is awesome.
  • 17: Put a Lid On It by Squirrel Nut Zippers. Maha loves this.
  • 16: One More Cup of Coffee by The White Stripes. White Stripes were cool when they covered old blues ditties and no one else knew about them.
  • 15: Without Me by Eminem. I would have imagined that some other Eminem song would be here but...this one is good too.
  • 14: Yeh Jafaa-e Gham Ka Chara by Abida Parveen. Abida Parveen's old Faiz kicks ass.
  • 13: Cherry Blosson Girl by Air. I don't really like this song. I mean, it is ok, at best.
  • 12: Sing It Again by Beck. Another great album and an amazing song. I will be listening to new Beck on my 80th birthday.
  • 11: California Stars by Billy Bragg and Wilco.This is one of my "summer" songs. Makes me want to go camping.
  • 10: I Want You by Bob Dylan. Short yet sweet.
  • 09: Hell is Chrome by Wilco. From the new CD, which I am still digesting. slowly.
  • 08: Good To Be On The Road Back Home Again by Cornershop. Cornershop is good, even though I am the ONLY one who thinks so.
  • 07: You Win Again by Hank Williams. Another Maha choice.
  • 06: Love Hurts by The Jayhawks. This is another cover and one of the few good ones.
  • 05: Lilac Wine by Jeff Buckley. I love this CD, his voice. This is one of my top 2 on that CD. Reminds me of writing web code for some reason...?
  • 04: Heer Waris Shah by Hamid Ali Bela. Amazing record.
  • 03: Take Your Mama Out by Scissor Sisters. The song for Summer 2004. Ms. Sepoy is sick of it already.
  • 02: Meda Ishq by Pathanay Khan. Pathanay Khan could have sung my grocery list and I would be entranced. I wish he was with us still.
